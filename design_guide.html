The C.R.A.P Principles

When designing a website, it’s very important to consider the experience of the user.

Gone are the days of Angelfire and Geocities pages, where a background of a GIF of a dancing hamster was considered to be part of a great website.

To create a great website nowadays, you need to follow four core principles of design called the C.R.A.P. principles.

Contrast

The user should be able to clearly distinguish different parts and elements of your website without much concentration. You want to be able to draw a user’s eye to different elements without them all visually blending like a polar bear in a snowstorm. Stick to two or three different contrasting elements, or you start to go back into hamster-dance-page territory.

Repetition

Using the same theme throughout the website will unify the contrasting elements into a cohesive website. Don’t use a different font for every paragraph and don’t use a different colour scheme on each page of the site.

Good Repetition 1

This box is teal with a medium border.

Good Repetition 2

This box is identical to the last one.

Good Repetition 3

This box is identical to the last one.

Bad Repetition 1

This box is blue with a medium border.

Bad Repetition 2

This box is green with a thin border and it is shorter than the previous one.

Bad Repetition 3

This box is red with a thick border and it is taller than the previous two.

Alignment

Creating a flow to the website is important. You want the user’s eye to follow a natural and logical path. If everything is out of alignment, like a car on the road, they’ll be all over the page and not know where to look. It can create user frustration and will deter users from your website.

Good Align 2

Proximity

Group like-things together and add some space between the groups so that the user knows they are separate from each other. If you have elements too close together it’s going to look like that collage you made in third grade and too cluttered.

Boxes are appropiately sized in the page.

Good Proximity 2

They are evenly spaced.

Good Proximity 3

The space between them is appropriate.

Bad Proximity 1

This box has too much surrounding space.

Bad Proximity 2

Their spacing is uneven.

Bad Proximity 3

These two have too little space between them.
